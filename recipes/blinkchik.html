Blinchik

Blinchiki are small, thin Russian pancakes often filled with savory or sweet fillings and then cooked or baked. They are a variation of blini or crepes, and the name "blinchiki" is a diminutive form, often used when referring to the filled versions.

In the West, blini traditionally refers to small savory pancakes made with leavened batter. In modern Russian, the term most often refers to pan-sized leavened thin pancakes, although smaller leavened pancakes are also called blini. Smaller and thicker pancakes (with several of them baked on one larger pan) are called oladyi.

Blintzes, called blinchiki (little blinis) in Russian are an offshoot of blini or crêpes. They are basically rolls based on thin pancakes usually made of wheat flour, folded to form a casing for various kinds of filing, typically cheese, fruit, or (in Russian cuisine) pre-fried minced meat, and then sautéed or baked

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (250 grams) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon (12 grams) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 cups (470-710 milliliters) milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons (30 milliliters) vegetable oil plus more or butter for the pan

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Slowly whisk in the milk until combined and no lumps remain. Add 2 cups (470 milliliters) milk for slightly thicker and easier to handle pancakes or up to 3 cups (710 milliliters) milk for thinner, more delicate pancakes.
  3. Whisk in the eggs, followed by the vegetable oil. If too thick, slowly add a little more milk. If too thin, slowly add a little more flour.
  4. Cover the bowl and allow to rest at room temperature for 15-30 minutes.
  5. Place a large nonstick skillet or pan over medium low heat. Lightly grease with oil or butter.
  6. Once thoroughly heated, add 1/4 – 1/3 cup (60-80 milliliters) batter to the center and immediately tilt the pan in a circle to coat the bottom in a thin layer.
  7. Cook until set on the top and the bottom turns golden, about 2 minutes.
  8. Flip and cook until the other side is golden, 30 seconds to 1 minute. Remove to plate. Rub the pan with more oil and repeat with remaining batter.
  9. Serve immediately with sour cream, jam, honey, and/or sweetened condensed milk.
